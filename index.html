A Divided America: Public Opinion Splits on Israel-Hamas War



A new national poll reveals that Americans are now evenly divided over whether Israel’s ongoing military campaign in Gaza is justified. The survey, conducted by Pew Research Center in February 2025, found that 39% of U.S. adults say Israel’s actions are justified, while 38% believe they are not. The remaining 23% are unsure or declined to answer.



This marks a significant shift from polling conducted in late 2023, when a majority of Americans expressed support for Israel’s response to the October 7 Hamas attack. The change comes amid mounting civilian casualties in Gaza, growing international criticism, and increased calls for a ceasefire from both U.S. lawmakers and global leaders.



Key Points


  • Americans are nearly evenly split on whether Israel’s military campaign in Gaza is justified: 39% say it is, 38% say it is not.

  • Public support for Israel’s actions has declined since late 2023, when a majority backed the response to the October 7 Hamas attack.

  • 23% of Americans remain unsure or declined to answer when asked about the justification for Israel’s actions.

  • The shift in opinion comes amid rising civilian casualties in Gaza and increased calls for a ceasefire from U.S. and international leaders.


Detailed Analysis


The Pew Research Center poll highlights a dramatic shift in American public opinion regarding the Israel-Hamas conflict. In late 2023, a majority of Americans supported Israel’s military response following the October 7 Hamas attack. However, by February 2025, support had dropped to 39%, with nearly as many (38%) saying Israel’s actions are not justified.



This change in sentiment is attributed to several factors. The ongoing conflict has resulted in significant civilian casualties in Gaza, drawing widespread media attention and humanitarian concern. International organizations and human rights groups have increasingly criticized the scale of Israel’s military operations, calling for restraint and a ceasefire.



The poll also found that public opinion is sharply divided along political lines. Among Republicans and Republican-leaning independents, a majority continue to view Israel’s actions as justified. In contrast, most Democrats and Democratic-leaning independents now say the campaign is not justified, reflecting growing divisions within the U.S. political landscape.





Age and education also play a role in shaping opinions. Younger Americans and those with higher levels of education are more likely to question the justification for Israel’s actions, while older adults and those with less formal education tend to be more supportive.



The survey indicates that 23% of Americans remain unsure or declined to answer, suggesting a significant portion of the public is either undecided or hesitant to take a firm stance on the issue. This uncertainty may be influenced by the complexity of the conflict and the rapidly evolving situation on the ground.





The U.S. government has faced increasing pressure from both domestic and international actors to push for a ceasefire and humanitarian aid in Gaza. Lawmakers from both parties have voiced concerns about the humanitarian impact, and public protests have called for an end to the violence.



Despite the divided opinions, the poll underscores a growing desire among Americans for a peaceful resolution to the conflict. Many respondents expressed support for diplomatic efforts and humanitarian assistance, reflecting a broader trend toward war-weariness and skepticism about prolonged military engagements in the region.





In summary, the Israel-Hamas war has become a deeply polarizing issue in the United States, with public opinion shifting in response to ongoing developments, rising casualties, and increased calls for peace.





Conclusion

The Israel-Hamas conflict has become a deeply polarizing issue in the United States, with public opinion shifting significantly since late 2023. The latest data shows Americans are now almost evenly divided on whether Israel’s military actions in Gaza are justified, reflecting growing concerns over civilian casualties and international pressure for a ceasefire.



  1. Public support for Israel’s actions has declined, with Americans now nearly evenly split on the issue.

  2. Political affiliation, age, and education level strongly influence individual perspectives on the conflict.

  3. A significant portion of Americans remain undecided, highlighting the complexity and sensitivity of the situation.

  4. There is increasing desire for diplomatic solutions and humanitarian aid, signaling war-weariness and hope for peace.
